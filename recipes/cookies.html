Cookies

Cookies are a beloved treat enjoyed around the world, known for their sweet flavor and chewy texture. Here's a simple recipe for classic chocolate chip cookies.


These cookies are made with a rich dough filled with chocolate chips. Start by creaming butter and sugar, then mix in eggs and vanilla. Combine dry ingredients separately, then mix with the wet ingredients. Fold in chocolate chips and bake until golden brown.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (226g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (220g) packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 cups (360g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups (340g) semi-sweet chocolate chips
  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
    • In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
    • Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in the vanilla extract.
    • In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt.
    • Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
    • Fold in the chocolate chips.
  2. Shape and Bake:
    • Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto ungreased baking sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
    • Bake in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.
    • Allow cookies to cool on the baking sheets for a few minutes before transferring to wire racks to cool completely.
  3. Serve:
    • Enjoy warm or store in an airtight container for up to a week.
